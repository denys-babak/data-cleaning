# 📊 Data Cleaning & Business Analysis with Eniac

## Project Overview

This project focuses on cleaning, validating, and analyzing Eniac's e-commerce sales data to uncover actionable business insights. The analysis investigates product discounts, seasonal purchasing behavior, product categorization, pricing distribution, and opportunities to improve data collection processes.

The goal was to transform raw, inconsistent data into a reliable dataset and use it to answer key business questions that support pricing, inventory, and marketing decisions.

## Business Questions

* How many products are being discounted?
* How large are discounts relative to product prices?
* How do seasonal events such as Black Friday and Christmas affect sales performance?
* How should products be classified into categories to improve reporting and analysis?
* What is the price distribution across product categories?
* How can Eniac improve its data collection practices?

## Key Findings

### Discount Strategy

* 94.7% of products were discounted at least once.
* Average discount: 17.1%
* Median discount: 12.9%
* Discounting is a core component of Eniac's pricing strategy.

However, larger discounts did not generate proportionally higher revenue.

* Products discounted by 5–10% generated the highest revenue (€9.83M).
* Discounts of 10–20% generated the highest number of units sold and orders.
* Discounts above 30% contributed relatively little revenue.

### Seasonality Analysis

Sales activity peaked during Black Friday and the Christmas/New Year period.

Key observations:

* Approximately 36% of all products were sold between November and January.
* Revenue per order increased during seasonal peaks.
* Average discounts were slightly lower during high-demand periods.

This indicates that seasonal demand, rather than deeper discounting, is the primary driver of increased sales.

### Product Categories

Revenue was highly concentrated among a small number of product groups.

* MacBooks
* iPhones
* Storage Products

These three categories generated nearly 80% of total revenue while requiring relatively modest discounts.

### Data Quality & Categorization

The project included extensive data cleaning and standardization efforts, including:

* Handling missing values
* Removing duplicates
* Standardizing product names
* Creating meaningful product categories
* Validating pricing and discount information

These improvements enabled more accurate reporting and analysis.

## Recommendations

1. Maintain moderate discounts (5–15%), which consistently drive strong revenue and sales volume.
2. Focus marketing and inventory planning around Black Friday and Christmas, where demand naturally increases.
3. Avoid excessive discounting, as large markdowns provide limited additional revenue.
4. Prioritize high-performing categories such as MacBooks, iPhones, and Storage products.
5. Improve data collection standards to ensure consistent product classification and higher-quality business reporting.

## Business Impact

The analysis demonstrates that Eniac can maximize revenue through strategic seasonal planning and moderate discounting rather than aggressive price reductions. By focusing on its strongest product categories and improving data quality, the company can make more informed commercial decisions and improve overall profitability.
